Regular Expressions

The name matching algorithm uses Regular Expressions like Perl or most UNIX tools (grep, vi, sed, awk, ...). Regular Expressions are very powerful but must not be confused with the primitive DOS filename patterns.

Regular Expressions vs. DOS filename pattens

Filename PattenReg.Exp..Meaning
?.Any Single Character
*.*Zero or More Characters

More Regular Expressions

Reg.Expr.Meaning
^Start
$End
.any character
a?'a' or no 'a'
a*zero or more 'a'
a+one or more 'a'
.+one or more arb. characters
a|b'a' or 'b'
[0-9]'0' or '1' or ... or '9'
()grouping of subexpressions

Examples

Reg.Expr.Matches...
TempAny Channel containing 'Temp'
Temp$Any Channel ending in 'Temp'
^RFQHRAny Channel starting with 'RFQHR'
Tin_[0-9]+_sig$Channels ending in 'Tin_<NUMBER>_sig'
^XYZSys_Temp[2-3]$'XYZSys_Temp2' or 'XYZSys_Temp3'
Pwr_(in)|(out)Channels containing 'Pwr_in' or 'Pwr_out'
